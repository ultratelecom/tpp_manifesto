MESSAGE FROM THE
POLITICAL LEADER
Fellow Tobagonians,
As we stand at the crossroads of our island's future, we
must reaffirm our commitment to effective and responsible
governance. For over 60 years, Tobagonians have upheld
the principle that no external government must do for us
what we can do for ourselves. This foundational belief has
guided our politics in our pursuit of a prosperous and self-
sufficient Tobago.

However, to truly unlock our island's full economic potential
and to realise our vision of making Tobago the "Greatest
Little Island on the Planet," we must embrace what it
truly means to govern our own affairs. This autonomy is
not merely a political aspiration; it is a necessity for our
progress and prosperity. It is through autonomy that
we can facilitate self-governance, allowing us to make
decisions that best reflect the needs and aspirations of
our people.

Autonomy is essential for safeguarding our cultural
identity, promoting economic development, and
enhancing political stability. It strengthens accountability
and provides us with the tools to address historical
inequalities that have long hindered our growth. By
gaining greater control over our own affairs, we can ensure
that the unique voice of Tobago is heard and respected.

As we move forward, let us unite in our pursuit of
constitutional autonomy. Let us work together to build
a future where every Tobagonian has the opportunity to
thrive, where our cultural heritage is preserved, and where
our island stands as a beacon of progress and innovation.

Together, we can achieve this vision. Together, we can
make Tobago the "Greatest Little Island on the Planet."




                                                                Farley Chavez Augustine
                                                                Political Leader
                                                                Tobago People's Party

3 FOCUS   AREA
  Participatory Governance

 Mechanisms for public consultation and participatory governance shall be established to ensure that
 Tobagonians have a direct voice in decision-making processes. This includes regular village hall meetings and
 the implementation of referenda on major policy decisions.


 KEY FOCUS AREA ACTIONS
 A TPP Government will:

 I. Ensure that diverse groups within society are involved in decision-making processes related to policy
     formulation and implementation, by utilising collaborative institutional and governance arrangements,
     such as Citizenship Councils and Co-administration Agreements.

 II. Ensure public engagement involves surveys, roundtable discussions, and working group discussions. The
     proceedings of these discussions will be documented and published as What-We-Heard Engagement
     Reports.

 III. Ensure that the information in What-We-Heard reports is used in developing and implementing policies,
     plans, programmes, and projects.

3 FOCUS AREA
  Teacher Training and Professional Development
Continuous investment in professional development to ensure educators are equipped with the latest teaching
methodologies and subject-matter knowledge. This will include workshops, seminars, and access to online
courses that foster a culture of lifelong learning among teachers.



KEY FOCUS AREA ACTIONS
A TPP Government will:

4 FOCUS  AREA
  Strengthen Early Childhood Education
Prioritise early childhood education by increasing access to quality pre-school programs. Develop standardised
guidelines and provide resources to ensure that early childhood educators are well-trained and that learning
environments are conducive to the developmental needs of young children.



KEY FOCUS AREA ACTIONS
A TPP Government will:

I. Upgrade the standards for preschool teachers, principals, and administrators to ensure that all teachers
    are certified to teach Mathematics and English Language.

II. Standardise the qualifications for early childhood teachers to include a certificate in Early Childhood
    Education and five O-levels, two of which must be CSEC Mathematics and English Language.

III. Register all private sector Early Childhood Education Centres to meet minimum operational and teaching
    standards.

5 FOCUS AREA
  Promote Inclusive Education
 Support inclusive education to ensure that students with disabilities and special needs have equal access to
 educational opportunities. This includes training teachers in special education, adapting learning materials,
 and improving school facilities to accommodate all students.




KEY FOCUS AREA ACTIONS
A TPP Government will:

I. Launch a pilot programme in inclusive education to include students
   with special needs in the regular classrooms with the necessary
   teacher support. Twelve primary schools and two secondary schools,
   including Speyside High School and Harmon’s SDA have been
   earmarked to participate in this pilot.

II. Convert all official government documents to Braille to ensure access
   to children and adult students who are visually impaired.




6 FOCUS AREA
  Enhance Parental and Community Involvement
 Foster partnerships between schools, parents, and the community to create a supportive educational
 environment. Encourage parental involvement in school activities and decision-making processes, and engage
 community stakeholders in initiatives that support educational development.




KEY FOCUS AREA ACTIONS
A TPP Government will:

I. Develop an online parent portal for the Tobago school district that will give parents
   real-time access to academic progress, attendance, and announcements. The portal will
   feature a user-friendly interface, communication tools for parent-teacher interaction, and
   security measures to safeguard data. Collaboration with IT specialists, educators, and
   parents will ensure the portal meets community needs.

II. Continue the Community Impact Programme to prepare parents who may face literacy
   and numeracy challenges to support their children at home.

  Resilient Assessment and Evaluation Systems
Establish comprehensive assessment and evaluation systems to monitor student progress and educational

1 FOCUS  AREA
  Family Support and Birth Rate Incentives
Introduce policies that support families and encourage higher birth rates. This can include providing parental
leave benefits, childcare support, and financial incentives for families with multiple children, to increase the
natural population growth rate.
          Tobago’s Peoples Party | Policy Mandate                                                          25




KEY FOCUS AREA ACTIONS
A TPP Government will:

I. Expand the Baby Box Programme to provide support for up to six months.
II. Introduce a Universal Child Benefit Programme for all children born in Tobago. The programme will
   provide $500/month in cash support for a child born in Tobago until the child reaches the age of 5.

III. Introduce the Nursery Care Programme for children from birth to preschool. The Tobago nursery
   care programme offers a nurturing, age-appropriate environment for children 0-3, focusing on early
   development through play-based activities. The programme will help children to develop cognitive,
   social, and motor skills with personalised caregiver attention. The facilities will have suitable toys and
   learning tools. Parents will participate in updates and development workshops, fostering a collaborative
   community.

IV. Establish Community Nursery Care Centres across Tobago.
V. Establish Workplace Nursery Care Centres in selected government buildings.

  Enhancement of Educational & Career Opportunities
Develop and expand educational institutions to attract both local and international students. This includes
offering scholarships and forming partnerships with global universities to enhance Tobago's academic
reputation, thereby increasing the student population and contributing to transient population growth and
associated longer-term residency.



KEY FOCUS AREA ACTIONS
A TPP Government will:

I. Establish the “University of Tobago” to retain Tobago students pursuing tertiary level studies and to
   attract regional and international students to boost the transient population.

II. Establish the Work for Tobago Programme to retain valuable human resources and specialist skills
   needed to drive Tobago's economic growth and development.
